Naptown Numbers

Let's have some quantitative context.

This site is about how policy discussions omit or misuse numbers. It's called Naptown Numbers because it began in reaction to a campaign for a transit tax to subsidize IndyGo, the city-bus company in Indianapolis.

In that campaign the typical proponent's attitude was something like "Improving Indianapolis transit would be worth an extra $100 in annual taxes." Note what's missing: the statement gives a price but not a quantity. $10 may be a reasonable price for apples if the quantity is ten pounds. Not so much if it's two apples.

In the case of IndyGo, the product is human transportation, not apples, so the relevant quantity was how many added passenger-miles the transit tax would enable IndyGo to provide. But despite a million-dollar propaganda campaign and dozens of "education" sessions it never provided an estimate of that quantity.
